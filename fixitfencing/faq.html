Materials & fencing types

  • Colorbond is steel — low-maintenance, termite-proof, non-combustible, and holds its colour without painting. Timber (treated pine or hardwood) gives a natural, warm appearance and can be stained or oiled. Colorbond needs less upkeep over time; timber gives a more traditional look. See Colorbond or hardwood for full detail.

  • Ezy Clip is a fencing system that combines treated pine palings with colour-coated steel posts and rails. You get the natural look of timber with the structural strength of steel — particularly popular in newer estates across Sydney. Read more.

  • Lap and cap has overlapping palings with a horizontal capping rail on top — a more decorative, finished look. Closed paling has each paling fixed directly side by side with no overlap — simpler, more affordable. Both provide full privacy.

  • H4 treated pine sleepers across the board. H4 is the highest treatment level for timber used in ground contact — protected against termites, rot, and fungal decay. Sleeper sizes from 200 mm to 600 mm, with optional galvanised steel brackets for added strength. See retaining walls.

  • “Better” depends on what you value. Hardwood is denser, stronger, and naturally durable without chemical treatment — the premium option. Treated pine is more affordable and still performs well when properly treated. Hardwood can be stained or oiled for a rich finish; treated pine weathers to grey over time unless maintained.

Costs, council & process

  • Honestly — it depends. Material, height, length, site conditions (slopes, access, old fence removal), and any gates required all change the number. We don’t give generic estimates; we’d rather visit your property and give you an accurate, detailed quote — for free. Call 0426 88 11 88 to arrange a measure and quote.

  • In most cases, residential fences up to 1.8 metres don’t require council approval in NSW. Exceptions: corner blocks, heritage areas, flood-prone land, fences on boundaries with public land, and pool fencing. We work across the Hills District, Parramatta, Blacktown and surrounding councils every day and will advise on what’s needed when we quote.
